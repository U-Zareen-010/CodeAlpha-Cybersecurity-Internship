Phishing Awareness Training

Introduction to Phishing

Phishing is a type of cyber attack where malicious actors disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to deceive individuals into divulging sensitive information. This can include emails that look like they are from your bank, employer, or a known service, urging you to click on links, download attachments, or provide personal information.

Understanding phishing tactics can help you avoid falling victim to these attacks.

Interactive Quiz

Question 1: What is a common sign of a phishing email?

Correct! Phishing emails often create a sense of urgency to prompt immediate action.

Explanation: Phishing emails often use urgency or threats to rush you into making a mistake, such as revealing personal information or clicking on harmful links.

Question 2: What should you do if you suspect a phishing attempt?

Correct! Reporting suspicious emails helps protect the organization and others from potential threats.

Explanation: Always report suspected phishing attempts to your IT department so they can take appropriate action to prevent any potential harm.

Question 3: Which of the following is a safe practice to avoid phishing?

Correct! Verifying links before clicking is crucial for avoiding phishing attacks.

Explanation: Always hover over links to see the actual URL and verify its legitimacy before clicking. Avoid clicking on links in unsolicited emails.

Question 4: What is a common phishing tactic used in emails?

Correct! Phishing emails often impersonate trusted organizations to deceive recipients.

Explanation: Phishers frequently impersonate trusted organizations or familiar contacts to make their emails seem more convincing.

Question 5: How can you verify the legitimacy of a suspicious email?

Correct! Contacting the sender through a known and separate communication channel is a good practice.

Explanation: Instead of replying to a suspicious email, use a trusted contact method to verify its authenticity. This helps ensure you are not communicating with the attacker.
